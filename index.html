Common Misconceptions

No, there are no horses involved. No, it is not like volleyball. No, the players cannot touch the bottom of the pool while playing.

Basic Mechanics

Each team has 6 field players and 2 goalies who guard a floating cage. It is similar to basketball in a sense where everyone goes to defense and everyone goes to offense. The objective is similar too, you work the ball around with your teammates until someone has the best shot opportunity.

Other Basic Rules

Field players can only touch the ball with one hand, goalies can use both. There are also power plays if a player gets removed from the game for a short time like hockey.

Game Length

A typical water polo game, depending on the age, has 4 quarters each lasting 7 minutes. There are short quarter breaks and a halftime break as well. The games usually last anywhere from 45 minutes to an hour. This picture shows a water polo player shooting to score (again, not touching the bottom of the pool).
